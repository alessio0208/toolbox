<div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Date</div>
<p><b>May 10</b> is the 130th day of the year (131st in <a href="/wiki/Leap_year" title="Leap year">leap years</a>) in the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a>. There are 235 days remaining until the end of the year. </p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=May_10&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/28_BC" title="28 BC">28 BC</a> – A <a href="/wiki/Sunspot" title="Sunspot">sunspot</a> is <a href="/wiki/Science_and_technology_of_the_Han_dynasty#Astronomical_observations" title="Science and technology of the Han dynasty">observed</a> by <a href="/wiki/Han_dynasty" title="Han dynasty">Han dynasty</a> <a href="/wiki/Chinese_astronomy" title="Chinese astronomy">astronomers</a> during the reign of <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Cheng_of_Han" title="Emperor Cheng of Han">Emperor Cheng of Han</a>, one of the earliest dated sunspot observations in China.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/AD_70" title="AD 70">AD 70</a> – <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Jerusalem_(AD_70)" class="mw-redirect" title="Siege of Jerusalem (AD 70)">Siege of Jerusalem</a>: <a href="/wiki/Titus" title="Titus">Titus</a>, son of emperor <a href="/wiki/Vespasian" title="Vespasian">Vespasian</a>, opens a full-scale assault on <a href="/wiki/Jerusalem" title="Jerusalem">Jerusalem</a> and attacks the city's Third Wall to the northwest.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1291" title="1291">1291</a> – Scottish <a href="/wiki/Nobles" class="mw-redirect" title="Nobles">nobles</a> recognize the authority of <a href="/wiki/Edward_I_of_England" title="Edward I of England">Edward I of England</a> pending the selection of a king.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1497" title="1497">1497</a> – <a href="/wiki/Amerigo_Vespucci" title="Amerigo Vespucci">Amerigo Vespucci</a> allegedly leaves <a href="/wiki/C%C3%A1diz" title="Cádiz">Cádiz</a> for his first voyage to the <a href="/wiki/New_World" title="New World">New World</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1503" title="1503">1503</a> – <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Columbus" title="Christopher Columbus">Christopher Columbus</a> visits the <a href="/wiki/Cayman_Islands" title="Cayman Islands">Cayman Islands</a> and names them <i>Las Tortugas</i> after the numerous <a href="/wiki/Turtle" title="Turtle">turtles</a> there.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1534" title="1534">1534</a> – <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Cartier" title="Jacques Cartier">Jacques Cartier</a> visits <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_(island)" title="Newfoundland (island)">Newfoundland</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1655" title="1655">1655</a> – England, with troops under the command of Admiral <a href="/wiki/William_Penn_(Royal_Navy_officer)" title="William Penn (Royal Navy officer)">William Penn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Venables" title="Robert Venables">General Robert Venables</a>, annexes <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">Jamaica</a> from Spain.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1688" title="1688">1688</a> – King <a href="/wiki/Narai" title="Narai">Narai</a> nominates his daughter <a href="/wiki/Sudawadi" title="Sudawadi">Sudawadi</a> to succeed him with <a href="/wiki/Constantine_Phaulkon" title="Constantine Phaulkon">Constantine Phaulkon</a>, Mom Pi and <a href="/wiki/Phetracha" title="Phetracha">Phetracha</a> acting as regents. The ensuing <a href="/wiki/Siamese_revolution_of_1688" title="Siamese revolution of 1688">revolution</a> leads to the <a href="/wiki/Ayutthaya_Kingdom" title="Ayutthaya Kingdom">Ayutthaya Kingdom</a> severing all ties with Europe.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1768" title="1768">1768</a> – <a href="/wiki/John_Wilkes" title="John Wilkes">John Wilkes</a> is imprisoned for writing an article for <i><a href="/wiki/The_North_Briton" title="The North Briton">The North Briton</a></i> severely criticizing <a href="/wiki/George_III_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="George III of the United Kingdom">King George III</a>. This action provokes rioting in London.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1773" title="1773">1773</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Great_Britain" title="Parliament of Great Britain">Parliament of Great Britain</a> passes the <a href="/wiki/Tea_Act" title="Tea Act">Tea Act</a>, designed to save the <a href="/wiki/British_East_India_Company" class="mw-redirect" title="British East India Company">British East India Company</a> by granting it a <a href="/wiki/Monopoly" title="Monopoly">monopoly</a> on the North American <a href="/wiki/Tea" title="Tea">tea</a> trade.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1774" title="1774">1774</a> – <a href="/wiki/Louis_XVI_of_France" title="Louis XVI of France">Louis XVI</a> and <a href="/wiki/Marie_Antoinette" title="Marie Antoinette">Marie Antoinette</a> become King and Queen of France.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1775" title="1775">1775</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">American Revolutionary War</a>: A small Colonial militia led by <a href="/wiki/Ethan_Allen" title="Ethan Allen">Ethan Allen</a> and Colonel <a href="/wiki/Benedict_Arnold" title="Benedict Arnold">Benedict Arnold</a> <a href="/wiki/Capture_of_Fort_Ticonderoga" title="Capture of Fort Ticonderoga">captures Fort Ticonderoga</a>.</li>
<li>1775 – American Revolutionary War: Representatives from the <a href="/wiki/Thirteen_Colonies" title="Thirteen Colonies">Thirteen Colonies</a> begin the <a href="/wiki/Second_Continental_Congress" title="Second Continental Congress">Second Continental Congress</a> in <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1796" title="1796">1796</a> – <a href="/wiki/War_of_the_First_Coalition" title="War of the First Coalition">War of the First Coalition</a>: <a href="/wiki/Napoleon_I_of_France" class="mw-redirect" title="Napoleon I of France">Napoleon I of France</a> wins a <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Lodi" title="Battle of Lodi">victory</a> against <a href="/wiki/Habsburg_Empire" class="mw-redirect" title="Habsburg Empire">Austrian</a> forces at <a href="/wiki/Lodi,_Lombardy" title="Lodi, Lombardy">Lodi</a> bridge over the <a href="/wiki/Adda_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Adda River">Adda River</a> in Italy. The Austrians lose some 2,000 men.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1801" title="1801">1801</a> – <a href="/wiki/First_Barbary_War" title="First Barbary War">First Barbary War</a>: The <a href="/wiki/Barbary_pirates" title="Barbary pirates">Barbary pirates</a> of <a href="/wiki/Tripoli" title="Tripoli">Tripoli</a> declare war on the United States of America.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1824" title="1824">1824</a> – The <a href="/wiki/National_Gallery" title="National Gallery">National Gallery</a> in London opens to the public.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1833" title="1833">1833</a> – The desecration of the grave of the viceroy of <a href="/wiki/Northern_and_southern_Vietnam" title="Northern and southern Vietnam">southern Vietnam</a> <a href="/wiki/L%C3%AA_V%C4%83n_Duy%E1%BB%87t" title="Lê Văn Duyệt">Lê Văn Duyệt</a> by Emperor <a href="/wiki/Minh_M%E1%BA%A1ng" title="Minh Mạng">Minh Mạng</a> provokes his <a href="/wiki/L%C3%AA_V%C4%83n_Kh%C3%B4i" title="Lê Văn Khôi">adopted son</a> to <a href="/wiki/L%C3%AA_V%C4%83n_Kh%C3%B4i_revolt" title="Lê Văn Khôi revolt">start a revolt</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1837" title="1837">1837</a> – <a href="/wiki/Panic_of_1837" title="Panic of 1837">Panic of 1837</a>: New York City banks fail, and <a href="/wiki/Unemployment" title="Unemployment">unemployment</a> reaches record levels.</li>
